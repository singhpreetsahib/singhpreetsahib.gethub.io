BASIC MEMBERSHIP

Fitness Equipment: Access to a range of fitness equipment such as cardio machines (treadmills, ellipticals, stationary bikes), weight machines, free weights (dumbbells, barbells), and functional training equipment (kettlebells, resistance bands, etc.). Group Exercise Classes: Basic memberships often include access to group fitness classes such as yoga, Pilates, Zumba, spinning, aerobics, and more. The availability of classes can vary depending on the gym.

PRIME MEMBERSHIP

Premium memberships often offer unlimited access to the gym's facilities, including workout areas, equipment, and possibly classes. Group Fitness Classes: Access to a range of group fitness classes such as yoga, spinning, HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training), pilates, and more. Personal Training Sessions: Some premium memberships might include a certain number of personal training sessions or consultations with fitness experts. Specialized Equipment: Access to specialized equipment or training zones that might not be available to basic members.

GOLD MEMBERSHIP

 old members might have access to additional workout areas, equipment, or facilities that basic members do not. Group Classes: Access to a variety of group fitness classes, which could include popular options like yoga, spinning, and strength training. Extended Hours: Gold members might have access to the gym during extended hours compared to basic members. Priority Booking: Priority booking for classes, personal training sessions, or other services that might require reservations. Locker and Towel Services: Complimentary locker usage and towel services for convenience.
